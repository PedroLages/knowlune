## Key Insights and Learnings

1. **Process commitments without automation fail consistently** — 36% follow-through rate on Epic 5 action items proves intent alone is insufficient. Automation is mandatory for compliance.

2. **Multiple review rounds (2-3) are the new baseline** — Every Epic 6 story needed iteration. Story estimates MUST budget for this overhead rather than treating it as exceptional.

3. **Test coverage targets are aspirational without strict enforcement** — Committed to ≥80% AC coverage in both Epic 5 and Epic 6 retrospectives. Achieved 33-50%. Target is meaningless without code-review-testing agent blocking <80% stories.

4. **Documentation consistency requires Definition of Done enforcement** — S01 has excellent lessons learned because the dev invested time. S02/S03 have placeholder text because there was no gate requiring completion. Must be part of DoD.

5. **Async timing bugs repeat without active prevention** — Race conditions, useEffect cleanup gaps, and fire-and-forget patterns appeared in all 3 stories despite being documented in Epic 5. Writing lessons in story files that nobody reads before the next story is equivalent to a verbal agreement.

6. **Review quality is high but catches issues late** — Design review caught WCAG contrast blockers in Round 2 that could've been caught in Round 1. Code review found zero-test critical functions. Earlier review trigger would reduce iteration cycles.

7. **Technical prep succeeded where process didn't** — Bug fixes (MilestoneGallery) and mechanical updates (CLAUDE.md) completed successfully. Manual process compliance (commit enforcement, test coverage, documentation) failed without enforcement.

8. **Infrastructure reuse accelerates delivery** — Leveraging Sonner, confetti, IndexedDB patterns from Epic 5 enabled 2-day turnaround for 3-story epic.

9. **Story 6.1's comprehensive documentation demonstrates best practice** — Eight specific lessons with context and solutions. This is the quality bar for all story documentation.

10. **WCAG violations persist across epics** — Touch targets, contrast ratios, aria-live regions, keyboard navigation — accessibility issues appear in 66% of stories despite being caught repeatedly in reviews. Suggests need for earlier accessibility validation.
